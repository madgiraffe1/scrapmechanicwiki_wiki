## Understanding the Threat: What Are Scrap Mechanic Bots?

**Scrap Mechanic bots**, often simply called robots, are the primary antagonists you'll encounter in the game's survival mode. These mechanical menaces aren't just static obstacles; they are dynamic threats that actively seek to dismantle your creations and eliminate you. They spawn both naturally across the landscape and during scheduled raids, forcing players to constantly adapt and fortify their bases. Their presence transforms the game from a purely creative sandbox into a challenging survival experience where resourcefulness and combat readiness are paramount.

These robotic foes aren't picky about their targets. They will attack players, animals, and even break objects within the game world. The damage they inflict isn't just to your health; they can systematically destroy your meticulously built structures. This object-breaking capability adds another layer of complexity, as you must consider the durability of your defenses when designing your base or vehicles.

### The Anatomy of an Attack: How Bots Damage Your Creations

When a Scrap Mechanic bot attacks, it's not just about hitting you or your vehicle. These enemies have a distinct mechanic for destroying objects. The likelihood of an object breaking depends on two main factors: the amount of damage the attack deals and the current durability of the object being hit. Stronger attacks are more likely to shatter weaker objects. While bots can inflict significant damage, there's a maximum durability level (typically 9) beyond which even the strongest attacks won't guarantee a break. This means robust, high-durability materials are essential for critical structures.

Bot attack damage can also vary based on proximity. Attacks delivered at closer range tend to inflict more harm, ranging from 1 to 9 points of damage depending on the bot's inherent strength. This encourages players to maintain distance or consider melee weapons that can withstand direct engagement. It's worth noting that in Creative Mode, bots spawned from Capsules are harmless and cannot damage players, allowing for safe experimentation and building.

### Bot Behavior: Detection and Aggression

Understanding how Scrap Mechanic bots perceive their environment is key to avoiding or managing encounters. Bots are highly sensitive to sound. They can hear noises emitted by Mechanics, such as footsteps, vehicle movement, or tool usage. Once a bot detects a Mechanic, it immediately becomes hostile and alerts all other bots within a 20-meter radius. This chain reaction can quickly turn a single encounter into a full-blown skirmish, emphasizing the importance of stealth or strategic engagement.

Beyond player-generated sounds, bots also react to other environmental noises, like falling objects. If they hear something drop, they will investigate the area for approximately two seconds before returning to their patrol. This offers creative players opportunities for distraction or luring bots into traps.

Interestingly, bots also exhibit hostility towards animals in the game. If no Mechanic is detected, they will actively attack creatures like Wocs and Glowbs. Wocs take priority over Glowbs in these animal-on-robot conflicts, a detail that can sometimes be exploited by players to divert bot attention.

### The Nimble Nuisance: Totebots

Totebots are often the first **Scrap Mechanic bots** you'll encounter. These green, humanoid robots are relatively fragile but can quickly overwhelm an unprepared Mechanic in groups. With 60 health and a normal attack damage of 15, they pose a consistent threat, especially during early game raids. Their 100% drop rate for Circuit Boards makes them a crucial source for electronic components, and they occasionally drop a Totebot Head, a unique decorative item. They can appear almost anywhere, making them a common sight. Upcoming red and yellow variants are rumored to have different stats and drops.

### The Haywire Harvester: Haybots

The Haybot is a more robust adversary than the Totebot, boasting 100 health. These large, scythe-wielding robots are easily recognizable and more dangerous. They have two main attacks: a "poke" dealing 20 damage and a more powerful "swing" that hits for 30 damage on normal difficulty. Haybots are a valuable source of raw materials, guaranteed to drop Scrap Metal. They also have a good chance of dropping Circuit Boards and Component Kits, making them a prime target for resource gathering once you can safely take them down. Like Totebots, they spawn frequently in the world and during raids.

### The Adhesive Aggressor: Tapebots

Tapebots are perhaps the most irritating of the common bots due to their high damage output and tendency to appear in crucial locations. Despite having only 40 health (Blue variant), their normal attack damage is a staggering 55, making them capable of quickly destroying vehicles and dealing heavy damage to players. Red Tapebots are even stronger, with 62 damage. They often spawn near or inside warehouses, acting as guardians of valuable loot. While they don't always drop items, they can provide Batteries and Component Kits. Upcoming yellow and green variants are expected to guard Grow Labs. Their high damage necessitates careful engagement, often favoring ranged attacks or strategic traps.

### The Colossal Cultivator: Farmbots

The Farmbot is the undisputed heavyweight champion of **Scrap Mechanic bots**. With an immense 1600 health, this agricultural behemoth is a true boss-level enemy. Farmbots possess multiple attack patterns, including a kick (10 damage), a swipe (35 damage), and a devastating pesticide cloud that deals 4 impact damage and 10 damage per second. Successfully defeating a Farmbot is a significant achievement, as they are guaranteed to drop a Warehouse Key, essential for progressing in the game. They also provide valuable Component Kits and Circuit Boards. Farmbots typically appear during the most challenging raids or at specific, dangerous locations.

## Tactics for Survival: Engaging Scrap Mechanic Bots

Engaging **Scrap Mechanic bots** requires a blend of preparation, strategic thinking, and quick reflexes. Here are some actionable tips for dealing with these mechanical menaces:

### General Engagement Strategies

*   **Scouting and Awareness:** Always be aware of your surroundings. Listen for bot sounds and scan the horizon, especially when traveling or establishing a new base. Early detection allows for better planning.
*   **Ranged Combat is Key:** Most bots are dangerous up close. Utilizing spudguns, potato cannons, or even long-range vehicle-mounted weapons can keep you safe while chipping away at their health.
*   **Exploit Line of Sight:** Bots need a clear line of sight to target you effectively. Use terrain, structures, or even your own custom-built defenses to break their line of sight, forcing them to reposition or allowing you to get free hits.
*   **Kiting and Movement:** Don't stand still! Constant movement, especially strafing or circle-strafing, makes you a harder target. For slower bots, kiting them around obstacles can allow you to attack while avoiding damage.
*   **Traps and Defenses:** For base defense, strategically placed spudgun turrets, sawblades, or even simple blockades can funnel bots into kill zones. Explosive barrels can be highly effective against groups or high-health bots like Farmbots.
