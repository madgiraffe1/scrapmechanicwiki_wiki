## Understanding the Scrap Mechanic Farmbot Threat

The world of *Scrap Mechanic* is full of creative building and adventurous survival, but lurking in the wilds is a formidable foe that can quickly turn your peaceful farming into a desperate fight for survival: the **Scrap Mechanic Farmbot**. These massive, red, six-legged robots are a significant threat to any Mechanic, capable of tearing through vehicles and structures with alarming ease. Understanding this dangerous adversary is crucial for anyone hoping to thrive in a world overrun by rogue machinery.

From their imposing presence to their devastating attacks, the **Scrap Mechanic Farmbot** demands respect and a well-thought-out approach. This guide will equip you with the knowledge and strategies needed to not only survive an encounter with these mechanical behemoths but to conquer them and claim their valuable rewards.

### Farmbot Overview and Statistics

The Farmbot is a large, red, six-legged robot equipped with a scythe arm, a pesticide gun, and a small drill on its underside. Its original purpose was likely agricultural, using its tools for planting, harvesting, and pest control, but now it's a relentless enemy.

Here's a quick look at the Farmbot's key statistics on Normal difficulty:

| Attribute       | Value (Normal)             | Value (Easy)               |
| :-------------- | :------------------------- | :------------------------- |
| Health          | 1600                       | 1600                       |
| Kick Damage     | 10                         | 5                          |
| Swipe Damage    | 35                         | 18                         |
| Pesticide Cloud (Impact) | 4                          | 2                          |
| Pesticide Cloud (Per Sec) | 10                         | 5                          |

These stats highlight the Farmbot's tanky nature with high health and its potential for significant damage, especially with its swipe and persistent pesticide cloud.

### Where Farmbots Roam: Spawn Locations

Knowing where to expect a Farmbot can be the difference between a planned engagement and a sudden ambush. These robots are not confined to a single area and can appear in various biomes, often catching unsuspecting players off guard.

Farmbots are commonly found in:

*   **Farmbot Scrapyards:** As the name suggests, these areas are prime Farmbot territory.
*   **Autumn Forests and Forests:** Dense woodland areas are particularly dangerous, as multiple Farmbots can spawn and use the environment to their advantage.
*   **Silo District:** Be prepared for encounters in this industrial zone.
*   **Ruin City:** The dilapidated urban landscape can also house these mechanical menaces.
*   **Wilderness (Rarely):** They can sporadically appear in the wild, sometimes surprisingly close to points of interest like Packing Stations.
*   **Warehouse Bases:** Occasionally, a Farmbot might spawn at the base of a four-story Warehouse.
*   **Raid Encounters:** During high-level raids, specifically Level 10 raids and the third night of Level 9 raids, Farmbots will actively seek out your base.

It's vital to remember that Farmbots can cut down large trees with their scythes if they are in the path to their target, further altering the environment during an encounter. They cannot swim but will walk underwater to reach their objective, making bodies of water less of a barrier than one might hope.

## Essential Strategies for Farmbot Encounters

Facing a Scrap Mechanic Farmbot head-on without preparation is often a recipe for disaster. These robots are fast, powerful, and can swiftly dismantle your creations. Developing effective strategies for combat and evasion is paramount to your survival.

### Weapon Effectiveness and Engagement Range

While the Farmbot is immune to Sledgehammer attacks, it is vulnerable to ranged weaponry. Here's a breakdown of how many shots various Spud Guns require to take down a Farmbot:

| Weapon Type     | Shots to Destroy (Normal) |
| :-------------- | :------------------------ |
| Spud Gun        | 58                        |
| Spud Shotgun    | 25                        |
| Spudling Gun    | 80                        |

The Spud Shotgun clearly offers the most efficient use of ammunition, requiring significantly fewer shots than the standard Spud Gun or Spudling Gun. When engaging, maintaining distance is key, as the Farmbot's kick and swipe attacks are devastating up close.

### Explosive Tactics: Canisters and Drills

For a quicker, more impactful approach, explosives are highly effective. Two Small Explosive Canisters or Large Explosive Canisters, combined with a few additional spud shots, can bring down a Farmbot.

**How to use explosives:**

1.  **Placement:** Place the canisters against a solid object or wall in the Farmbot's predicted path.
2.  **Luring:** Lure the Farmbot towards the explosives.
3.  **Detonation:** Shoot one of the canisters just as the Farmbot reaches them to trigger a chain reaction.

Another creative, yet risky, method involves using the Lift tool. Player experience suggests that attaching an assortment of drills to the underside of a plate of blocks and dropping it onto a Farmbot can inflict significant damage. This requires precise timing and positioning but can be incredibly satisfying.

### Evasion and Kiting Techniques

Sometimes, direct confrontation isn't the best option. The Farmbot matches the Mechanic's sprint speed, making simple running away difficult. However, kiting can be an effective strategy, especially with faster-firing weapons.

**Kiting strategy:**

1.  **Get Attention:** Lure the Farmbot to focus on you.
2.  **Sprint and Shoot:** Sprint away, occasionally turning to fire a few shots with your ranged weapon.
3.  **Weapon Choice:** This method is most effective with the Spud Shotgun or Spudling Gun due to their higher damage output or sustained fire.

Be cautious in dense forests, as multiple Farmbots can easily ambush you. If you're on foot and surrounded, seeking higher ground can offer a temporary reprieve.

## Advanced Tactics and Environmental Exploitation

The Scrap Mechanic Farmbot, while powerful, can be outsmarted by utilizing the environment and clever vehicle designs. These advanced tactics can turn the tide in your favor, even against multiple Farmbots.

### Vehicle Combat and Ramming

Driving a heavy-duty vehicle can be a viable, albeit risky, strategy. A sufficiently heavy vehicle can damage a Farmbot and cause it to ragdoll, allowing for repeated ramming. However, this approach carries a significant risk: the Farmbot's powerful swipe attacks can easily destroy vehicles with low durability. Investing in robust, armored vehicles is essential if you plan to engage in vehicular combat. Remember that their attacks can even damage blocks through walls, so basic barriers might not offer complete protection.

### Using the Terrain to Your Advantage

The environment offers several opportunities to gain an edge against the Farmbot:

*   **Silo District Canals:** In the Silo District, you can lure a Farmbot into an empty canal. This traps the Farmbot, allowing you to safely shoot it from above without fear of retaliation, as it cannot reach you.
*   **Desert Oil Pools:** Leading a Farmbot into an oil pool in the desert can also immobilize it. The steep sides of the pool prevent it from walking out, and being submerged in liquid prevents it from using its pesticide gun.
*   **Elevated Positions:** While an elevated shelter won't protect you from the pesticide gun's area-of-effect damage, standing underneath something can block the direct impact of the pesticide shot. This offers partial protection but isn't a foolproof defense for prolonged engagements.
*   **Defensive Walls (Community Reports):** Community reports suggest that building a wall with a 4x4 hole in it might allow players to safely shoot a Farmbot on the other side. This strategy requires further confirmation and testing but highlights the creative defensive building possibilities in *Scrap Mechanic*.

These environmental traps can minimize direct confrontation, conserving resources and reducing risk.

## Farmbot Drops and Valuable Loot

Defeating a Scrap Mechanic Farmbot is not just about survival; it's also about the valuable resources it drops. These items are crucial for progression and crafting in *Scrap Mechanic*.

Each Farmbot kill guarantees a Warehouse Key and offers a chance at additional crafting components.

| Item              | Chance   | Quantity |
| :---------------- | :------- | :------- |
| Warehouse Key     | 100%     | 1        |
| Component Kit     | 66.7%    | 2-3      |
| Circuit Board     | 33.3%    | 1        |

**Note on Warehouse Keys:** According to the official *Scrap Mechanic* Wiki, future updates may change the drop mechanics for Warehouse Keys. It's anticipated that Warehouse Keys will no longer drop directly from Farmbots in a major Survival update, being replaced by Circuit Boards as the primary drop. This suggests a new method for obtaining Warehouse Keys will be introduced. Always check the latest game updates for changes to loot tables. ## The Evolution and Lore of the Farmbot

Beyond its combat prowess, the Farmbot has an interesting history and some lesser-known facts that add to its lore within *Scrap Mechanic*.

### Historical Context and Trivia

*   **Original Name:** Farmbots were originally known as "Agribots" during early Beta or late Alpha stages of the game's development.
*   **Debut Trailer:** They made an appearance in the game's debut trailer, chasing the Mechanic, indicating they had rudimentary AI even then.
*   **Early Models:** Game files contain early Farmbot models and textures, including variations with green bodies and orange eyes, or yellow bodies with red eyes.
*   **"Boss Bot":** Due to their mini-boss status and formidable nature, Farmbots are often unofficially referred to as "Boss Bots" by the community.
*   **Melted Farmbots:** Farmbot Scrapyards feature melted, inactive Farmbots. Interestingly, these appear to be based on the older Alpha Farmbot model, with noticeable differences, particularly in the scythe arm.
*   **Unused Attacks:** The game files contain animations and Lua code for an unused charge/breach attack. This behavior can sometimes be observed when a Farmbot briefly moves away from the player before returning, as if trying to initiate a charge that never quite fires.
*   **Purpose:** The Farmbot's design clearly indicates its original purpose was agricultural. Its drill was likely for planting seeds, its scythe for harvesting crops, and its pesticide gun for spraying crops.

### Beta Development History

The Farmbot has undergone significant changes throughout *Scrap Mechanic*'s Beta development, primarily impacting its health and behavior:

| Version | Changes Implemented                                                                                                |
| :------ | :----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 0.5.0   | Health increased from 400 to 1600. Fixed a bug that could cause Farmbots to become invulnerable.                   |

These historical changes underscore the developers' commitment to balancing the game and refining the challenge posed by the Scrap Mechanic Farmbot, making it the iconic enemy it is today.

## Frequently Asked Questions About the Scrap Mechanic Farmbot

### Q: What is the best weapon to use against a Scrap Mechanic Farmbot?
A: While all Spud Guns can damage a Farmbot, the Spud Shotgun is generally considered the most efficient, requiring only 25 shots on Normal difficulty to destroy it. Its high burst damage makes it ideal for quicker engagements.

### Q: Can Farmbots swim?
A: No, Farmbots cannot swim due to their large size. However, they can walk underwater to reach their target, so bodies of water do not provide a safe escape or barrier from a determined Scrap Mechanic Farmbot.

### Q: What loot can I expect from defeating a Farmbot?
A: Every Farmbot guarantees one Warehouse Key. Additionally, there's a 66.7% chance to get 2-3 Component Kits and a 33.3% chance to get a Circuit Board. Be aware that future updates may change the Warehouse Key drop mechanics.

### Q: Are there any environmental weaknesses of the Scrap Mechanic Farmbot?
A: Yes, Farmbots can be lured into specific environmental traps. In a Silo District, they can be trapped in empty canals. In a Desert, they can fall into oil pools, where the steep sides prevent escape and the liquid prevents them from using their pesticide gun.
