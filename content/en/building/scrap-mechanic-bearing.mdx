## The Core of Motion: What is a Scrap Mechanic Bearing?

Ever wondered how to make your Scrap Mechanic creations come alive with movement? The **Scrap Mechanic bearing** is the fundamental component that unlocks dynamic motion, from spinning wheels to complex mechanisms. This interactive part is the cornerstone for any aspiring inventor looking to add rotation to their builds. Understanding the **Scrap Mechanic bearing** is crucial for any aspiring inventor in the game, enabling everything from basic vehicles to intricate contraptions.

At its heart, a bearing in Scrap Mechanic acts as a pivot point. When placed, the block it attaches to becomes the stationary base, while any object connected directly onto the bearing gains the ability to rotate freely. This simple principle forms the basis for nearly all moving parts in the game. It's an interactive item that brings your static creations to life.

However, there's a key interaction to remember: when your creation is on a Lift, all connected bearings will be temporarily locked. This prevents unintended movement during construction. Once the Lift is removed, physics takes over, allowing the bearings to rotate freely. If a bearing is linked to a control part, its rotation will only activate when the control is engaged, and its connected parts won't freely respond to physics until that control is released. This behavior is especially important for designing stable and predictable vehicles, where bearings are indispensable for both wheel rotation and steering systems.

## Acquiring Bearings: Finding and Crafting Your Rotational Essentials

Whether you're just starting your Scrap Mechanic journey or are a seasoned builder, knowing how to get your hands on bearings is essential. Fortunately, there are a couple of reliable methods for obtaining this crucial component, allowing you to choose the approach that best suits your current game progression and resource availability.

### Scavenging for Bearings

For those who prefer to explore and gather, bearings can be found in the world. They are often dropped by **Damaged Crates** when destroyed, offering a chance to acquire them through combat or environmental interaction. Additionally, players report finding bearings within **Ruin Chests**, scattered across the game's landscape. These chests often contain valuable components, making them worth seeking out for a steady supply of various parts, including bearings.

### Crafting Bearings: Your Workshop Solution

If scavenging isn't yielding enough results, or if you prefer a more controlled acquisition method, you can craft bearings directly. The crafting process is straightforward and requires a common resource: Scrap Metal Blocks. This makes it an accessible option once you have a basic crafting setup.

Here's what you'll need to craft a single Scrap Mechanic bearing:

| Component         | Quantity |
| :---------------- | :------- |
| Scrap Metal Block | 5        |

The crafting time for a bearing is generally 15 seconds. However, if you've leveled up your crafting skills, specifically to Level 5, this time can be reduced to a swift 9 seconds. You can craft bearings using either the **Mini Craftbot** or the standard **Craftbot**, providing flexibility depending on your base setup.

## Unleashing Control: How to Operate Your Bearings

The true power of a Scrap Mechanic bearing comes from its ability to be controlled. Simply allowing free rotation is useful, but integrating bearings with control parts opens up a world of possibilities for complex, functional machinery. The game provides several ways to connect and manipulate bearings, each offering unique advantages depending on the desired outcome.

All bearing control begins with the **Connect Tool**. This essential tool allows you to link a bearing to a control part. A particularly useful feature of the Connect Tool is the ability to toggle a connected bearing's rotation direction by simply right-clicking on it. This simple action provides immense flexibility in your designs.

### Engine Power

For continuous, powerful rotation, connecting your bearings to an engine is the way to go. Both **Gas Engines** and **Electric Engines** can provide the necessary torque to spin bearings, making them ideal for wheels, propellers, or any component requiring constant motion. Engines offer a straightforward "on/off" control for rotation, perfect for applications where sustained movement is key.

### Driver's Seat Precision

When it comes to vehicle steering or specific angular movements, the **Driver's Seat** offers a more nuanced approach. Standard Driver's Seats allow bearings to turn approximately 27 degrees in either direction, providing basic steering functionality for cars and other mobile contraptions. However, for advanced builders, the **Driver's Seat 5** is a game-changer. This upgraded version allows you to configure both the turn speed and the maximum turn angle, offering much greater precision and customization for your vehicle's handling.

### Advanced Controller Logic

For the ultimate in precise and programmable control, the **Controller** is indispensable. This powerful logic gate enables you to define specific starting angles for your bearings and even program complex rotation sequences. With a Controller, you can create intricate robotic arms, automated doors, precise aiming systems, and more. It offers unparalleled flexibility for designing mechanisms that require specific movements at specific times.

To help you choose the right control method, here's a comparative overview:

| **Electric Engine**| Continuous rotational force  | Simple on/off, powerful                   | Wheels, propellers, constant spinning mechanisms      |
| **Driver's Seat**| Basic directional input      | 27° turn angle (standard)                 | Vehicle steering, simple pivoting parts               |

## Optimization Tips for Bearing Performance

While the **Scrap Mechanic bearing** is incredibly powerful, optimizing its placement and usage can significantly improve the performance, stability, and reliability of your creations. A well-designed bearing system can mean the difference between a smooth-running machine and a wobbly, physics-defying disaster.

Here are some tips to get the most out of your bearings:

1.  **Stable Mounting:** Always ensure the base block a bearing is placed on is securely attached and stable. A wobbly base will lead to a wobbly rotating part. Reinforce attachment points, especially for heavy or fast-moving components.
2.  **Weight Distribution:** Pay close attention to the weight distribution of the object rotating on the bearing. Uneven weight can cause excessive strain, leading to instability or unexpected physics interactions. Try to balance the load around the bearing's axis of rotation.
3.  **Minimize Unnecessary Bearings:** While bearings are great, sometimes less is more. Each bearing adds to the physics calculations. If a part doesn't need to rotate independently, consider rigid attachment instead.
4.  **Avoid Clipping:** Ensure that the rotating part does not clip into the stationary base or other parts during its full range of motion. Clipping can cause physics glitches, unexpected forces, or even break your creation. Use the "Connect Tool" to set precise rotation limits with controllers if needed.
5.  **Utilize Controllers for Precision:** For critical movements or delicate machinery, always opt for a Controller. It allows for setting exact starting angles, rotation speeds, and sequences, which is far more reliable than manual input or simple engine power for complex tasks.
6.  **Troubleshooting Bearing Issues:** If a bearing isn't behaving as expected, first check its connection with the Connect Tool. Ensure it's linked to the correct control part and that its rotation direction is set appropriately. If physics issues persist, try rebuilding the section or simplifying the design. Sometimes, simply re-placing the bearing can resolve minor glitches.
7.  **Consider Material Properties:** While the bearing itself has fixed properties (like non-flammable), the materials you build your rotating components from can impact overall weight and durability. Factor this into your designs, especially for vehicles that might take damage.

By carefully considering these optimization tips, you can transform your raw ideas into robust, functional, and impressive Scrap Mechanic contraptions that leverage the full potential of the **Scrap Mechanic bearing**. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: What is a Scrap Mechanic bearing primarily used for?
A **Scrap Mechanic bearing** is an interactive part used to create rotational movement in your contraptions. It's essential for anything that needs to spin or pivot, such as vehicle wheels, steering mechanisms, robotic arms, automated doors, and other dynamic machinery.

### Q2: How do I craft a Scrap Mechanic bearing?
You can craft a bearing using 5 Scrap Metal Blocks. The crafting process takes 15 seconds (or 9 seconds at Craftbot Level 5) and can be done at either a Mini Craftbot or a standard Craftbot.

### Q3: Can I control the speed and angle of my bearings?
Yes, you can control the speed and angle. While basic Driver's Seats offer a fixed 27-degree turn, the Driver's Seat 5 allows you to configure both the turn speed and the maximum angle. For even more precise and programmable control, the Controller lets you define starting angles and complex rotation sequences.

### Q4: Why are my bearings locked when I'm building?
Bearings are automatically locked when your creation is placed on a Lift. This prevents unintended movement during construction. Once the Lift is removed, the bearings will become active and respond to physics or any connected control parts.
