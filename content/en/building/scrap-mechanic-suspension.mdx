## Designing with Suspension in Mind: Tips and Best Practices

Integrating **Scrap Mechanic suspension** effectively goes beyond simply attaching the parts. Thoughtful design can make all the difference between a stable workhorse and a physics-defying nightmare.

### Placement and Integration

*   **Even Distribution:** Always aim for an even distribution of suspension units across your vehicle. Most four-wheeled vehicles will benefit from one Sport Suspension per wheel. For larger, heavier vehicles, you might consider dual suspension on each corner or additional units in the middle.
*   **Bearing Limitations:** A crucial note from player experience and official guidelines is that suspensions cannot be placed directly on Bearings, nor can Bearings be placed on suspensions. Both components serve to join other objects, and their direct combination is disallowed by the game's physics engine. Plan your chassis and wheel connections accordingly.
*   **Chassis Connection:** Connect your Sport Suspensions directly to your vehicle's chassis. The wheels then attach to the suspension units. This creates the necessary dampening effect.

### Stiffness Settings and Vehicle Types

Adjusting the stiffness of your **Scrap Mechanic suspension** is a powerful tool for customization. Here’s a general guide:

| Vehicle Type       | Recommended Stiffness | Rationale                                                                                             |
| :----------------- | :-------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Light Buggies**  | Lower (e.g., 4-7)     | Allows for more flex and shock absorption over bumps, providing a smoother ride for lighter builds.     |
| **Off-Road Trucks**| Medium (e.g., 7-10)   | Balances bounce for rough terrain with enough firmness to prevent excessive body roll and tipping.      |
| **Heavy Haulers**  | Higher (e.g., 10-15+) | Essential for supporting heavy loads, preventing sag, and maintaining stability during transport.       |
| **Race Cars**      | High (e.g., 12-15+)   | Minimizes body roll and maintains tire contact during high-speed turns, crucial for competitive racing. |

These are general guidelines; the best stiffness setting for your vehicle will ultimately come down to experimentation. Factors like vehicle weight, center of gravity, and desired handling characteristics all play a role.

## Troubleshooting Common Suspension Issues

Even with the best planning, you might encounter issues with your **Scrap Mechanic suspension**. Here are some common problems and their solutions:

### Common Suspension Issues & Solutions

| Issue                               | Potential Cause                                     | Solution                                                                                                   |
| :---------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Vehicle Tips Easily**             | Too soft suspension, high center of gravity, narrow wheelbase. | Increase stiffness, lower center of gravity, widen wheelbase, add more suspension units.                    |
| **Excessive Bouncing/Wobbling**     | Suspension too soft, uneven weight distribution, too few suspension units. | Increase stiffness, redistribute weight, add more suspension units for better support.                       |
| **Vehicle Sags/Bottoms Out**        | Suspension too weak for vehicle weight, insufficient stiffness. | Increase stiffness, add more suspension units, consider a stronger suspension type (if available, or modded). |
| **Stiff Ride, Poor Terrain Adhesion**| Suspension too stiff for terrain, too little bounce. | Decrease stiffness, ensure adequate wheel travel, use larger wheels for better ground clearance.             |
| **Suspension Not Working**          | Incorrect placement, interference with bearings.    | Ensure suspensions are directly connected to chassis, not bearings. Check for obstructions.                 |

Remember, *Scrap Mechanic* physics can be quirky. Sometimes, a slight adjustment to weight distribution or even the type of material used for your chassis can have an impact on how your **Scrap Mechanic suspension** performs. Don't be afraid to experiment!

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: What is the primary purpose of a Scrap Mechanic suspension?
**A1:** The primary purpose of a Scrap Mechanic suspension, particularly the Sport Suspension, is to add bounce and stability to vehicles. It helps prevent vehicles from easily tipping over, especially when traversing uneven terrain or making sharp turns, significantly improving handling and control.

### Q2: Can I place a Sport Suspension directly onto a Bearing in Scrap Mechanic?
**A2:** No, you cannot place a Sport Suspension directly onto a Bearing, nor can a Bearing be placed on a suspension. This is because both components are designed to join other objects together, and the game's physics engine prevents their direct combination. You must connect them indirectly, typically by attaching the suspension to the chassis and the wheel to the suspension.

### Q3: How do I adjust the stiffness of my Scrap Mechanic suspension?
**A3:** The stiffness of your Sport Suspension can be adjusted by upgrading it. The base suspension (Level 1) has a stiffness of 4. Upgrading to Level 2 (which requires 1 Component Kit) increases the stiffness to 7. Further upgrades, if available, would likely offer even higher stiffness settings, allowing you to fine-tune your vehicle's handling.

### Q4: What materials are needed to craft a Sport Suspension?
**A4:** To craft a Sport Suspension in Scrap Mechanic, you will need 5 Metal Block 2 and 2 Crude Oil. These materials can then be combined at a Craftbot, with crafting times varying based on the Craftbot's level.
