## Welcome to the World of Scrap Mechanic!

Stepping into Scrap Mechanic for the first time can feel like being handed a box of LEGOs and told to build a spaceship – exciting, but a little overwhelming. This comprehensive **Scrap Mechanic beginner guide** is designed to cut through the initial confusion, helping you grasp the core mechanics so you can spend less time fumbling and more time unleashing your creativity. Whether you're keen to build an epic vehicle or just survive your first few nights, understanding the fundamentals is key. By the end of this guide, you’ll have a solid foundation to conquer the world of Scrap Mechanic.

## Core Gameplay Mechanics: Your Toolkit for Creation

Scrap Mechanic is all about building, but before you can construct your magnum opus, you need to understand how to interact with the world and its components.

### Basic Controls: Navigating Your Environment

Getting around and manipulating objects are your first steps. The game uses standard WASD controls, but a few other keys are crucial for efficient gameplay.

| Action                 | Key/Mouse Input              | Description                                                          |
| :--------------------- | :--------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Move Forward/Backward  | W / S                        | Standard movement                                                    |
| Strafe Left/Right      | A / D                        | Standard sideways movement                                           |
| Look Around            | Mouse                        | Control your camera view                                             |
| Jump                   | Space Bar                    | Leap over obstacles or reach higher ground                           |
| Crouch                 | Hold Ctrl                    | Sneak through tight spaces or duck under objects                     |

| Interact               | E                            | Use objects, enter vehicles, open menus (e.g., engine settings)      |

### Inventory and Hotbar: Managing Your Resources

Your inventory is where you store all the blocks, parts, tools, and interactive components you collect. Pressing `I` opens it up. You can hover your mouse over any item to see its name, weight, and function.

To use an item quickly, drag it from your inventory down to the hotbar at the bottom of your screen. Once it's on your hotbar, you can select it using your mouse wheel without having to open your full inventory. This is essential for fast-paced building and survival situations.

### Building Essentials: Blocks, Parts, and Interactive Components

Scrap Mechanic categorizes building objects into three main types, each with unique properties and uses. Mastering these is fundamental to any **Scrap Mechanic beginner guide**.

#### 1. Blocks

Blocks are the foundational materials of your creations, such as stone, wood, or metal. They are versatile and can be scaled to create larger structures.

*   **Placing**: Left-click to place individual blocks.
*   **Deleting**: Right-click to delete a single block.
*   **Scaling**: Hold down the left mouse button and drag to scale a block. Press `Q` to switch between horizontal and vertical scaling.
*   **Mass Deletion**: Hold down the right mouse button and mark an area to delete multiple blocks at once.
*   **Identification**: In your inventory, blocks have a blue line underneath them.

#### 2. Parts

Parts are decorative and have unique appearances but no specific functional properties. They are perfect for adding flair and detail to your builds.

*   **Placement**: Left-click to place.
*   **Deletion**: Right-click to delete.
*   **Rotation**: Press `Q` to rotate a part. The number of rotation steps varies depending on the part's design.
*   **Precision Placement**: Hold down the left mouse button and drag to place parts with greater precision on surfaces.

#### 3. Interactive Parts

These are the heart of advanced Scrap Mechanic creations, each possessing unique features that bring your builds to life.

## Essential Tools: Connecting and Manipulating

Beyond your basic building components, a couple of tools are indispensable for any aspiring mechanic.

### The Connect Tool: Bringing Creations to Life

Many interactive parts need to be connected to function together. The Connect Tool is how you establish these relationships.

1.  **Select the Connect Tool** from your toolbar.
2.  **Identify Connection Points**: Interactive parts will show glowing connection points when you hover over them.
3.  **Connect**: Hold the left mouse button on one connection point and drag the line to another connection point on the same structure. Release the mouse button to create the connection.
4.  **Adjust Direction**: For parts like bearings, you can right-click with the Connect Tool to change their spin direction.
5.  **Activate**: Press `E` on connected interactive parts (like an engine or driver's seat) to access their settings or use them.

### The Lift: Your Mobile Workshop

The Lift is arguably one of the most critical tools in your arsenal. Every player has one, and it's invaluable for building and recovering vehicles.

*   **Physics Control**: The Lift freezes the physics of your creation, allowing you to build without gravity interfering and keeping your project separated from the ground.
*   **Recovery**: If your vehicle tips over, select the Lift, point it at your creation, and left-click. It will pick up the entire structure, allowing you to reposition it safely.
*   **Elevation**: Use the arrow keys on your keyboard while the Lift is active to raise or lower your creation, making it easier to build components from underneath.
*   **Deployment**: Once your creation is ready, left-click to place it down, then right-click to pick up the Lift and let physics take over.

## Your First Vehicle: A Basic Scrap Mobile

Let's put some of these building concepts into practice with a simple vehicle build. This is a common first project in any **Scrap Mechanic beginner guide**.

1.  **Place the Lift**: Start by placing your Lift on a flat surface.
2.  **Build the Base**: Select a metal block (or any block you prefer) and scale it to a suitable size for your vehicle's base on top of the Lift.
3.  **Add Driver's Seat**: Place a Driver's Seat on your base. Use `Q` to rotate it so it faces the direction you want to drive.
4.  **Install Engine**: Place a Gas Engine (or Electric Engine) somewhere on your base.
5.  **Add Bearings for Steering**: For steering, place two bearings at the front corners of your vehicle's base.
6.  **Add Wheels**: Place a wheel on each of the front bearings. For the rear, you can either add two more bearings and wheels, or simply attach wheels directly to the frame if you don't need powered rear wheels for this basic model.
7.  **Elevate the Lift**: Use the arrow keys to raise the Lift, giving you space to work underneath.
8.  **Connect Components with the Connect Tool**:
    *   **Driver's Seat to Engine**: Connect the Driver's Seat to the Gas Engine. This allows you to control the engine from the seat.
    *   **Engine to Wheels**: Connect the Gas Engine to *all* the wheels you want powered.
    *   **Driver's Seat to Front Bearings**: Connect the Driver's Seat to the two front bearings. This will enable steering.
    *   **Adjust Rotation**: Right-click on the front bearings with the Connect Tool to ensure they turn the wheels in the correct direction for steering. Also, check that powered wheels rotate forward.
9.  **Adjust Engine Speed**: Press `E` on the Gas Engine. Start with a low speed setting (e.g., second gear) to prevent your small vehicle from flipping.
10. **Test Drive**: Right-click to remove the Lift. Hop into your Driver's Seat (press `E`) and take your new scrap mobile for a spin!

This basic vehicle can be infinitely expanded with suspension, thrusters, and more. There's no wrong way to build; your imagination is the only limit!

## Navigating the Survival World: Your Early Adventures

While the creative mode is pure sandbox fun, the survival mode adds a layer of challenge and progression. New players often wonder, "What do I do next?" in this expansive world.

### Initial Survival Steps

Community reports and player experience suggest a clear path for your first hours:

1.  **Crash Site Basics**: Upon spawning, explore around your crashed ship. Find the nearby pond to gather water and use a bucket to extinguish the fires on your ship.
2.  **Powering Up**: Follow the crash site debris to a large building to retrieve a battery. Bring it back to your ship to activate basic functions.
3.  **Finding the Mechanic's Garage**: Head back to the large building and locate the road. Follow this road. It will lead you to the Mechanic's Garage, which is a crucial hub for crafting and progression.
4.  **Farming**: Once at the garage, establish a small farm. Be aware that planting more than nine crops together can attract enemy robot waves (referred to as "farmbots"), so start small.
5.  **The Packing Station**: Continue following the road to find the Packing Station. Investigate the cliffside directly behind it.

### Progression Milestones (Player Experience)

According to various community discussions, players typically progress through several key milestones:

*   **Early Game**:
    *   Learn to grow crops.
    *   Power up your scrap craftbot.
    *   Loot the first ruined buildings and survive encounters with Haybots (those orange, pitchfork-wielding robots).
    *   Build a basic "jalopy" vehicle for transport, even if it's not perfect.
    *   Find and power up the Mechanic's Garage.
    *   Craft your first Craftbot.
*   **Mid Game**:
    *   Establish a home base and set up a beacon (e.g., leaving an item in a respawn bag) to easily find your way back.
    *   Explore to find essential resources like cotton, oil, beeswax, and corn.
    *   Build a better car for carrying more loot.
    *   Craft a Refinebot to process raw materials.
    *   Develop automated systems for harvesting trees and stone.
    *   Defend your farm from midnight raids.
    *   Discover the Packing Plant and understand its function.
    *   Locate the Trader for resource exchange.
    *   Rescue captive farmers and build a vehicle capable of transporting them.
*   **Late Game**:
    *   Farm, build, and harvest repeatedly to expand your operations.
    *   Craft advanced bots like the Cookbot, Spudguns, and Dressbot.
    *   Venture further out to find Tapebots and Warehouses, indicating more advanced areas and challenges.

### Navigation Challenges

There is no in-game map in Scrap Mechanic's survival mode, so navigation relies heavily on landmarks. Roads are your best friends for finding key locations. Some players use lamps from scrap towns to create directional arrows at crossroads, helping them find their way back home. Exploration and experimentation are highly encouraged, as this sandbox game thrives on player-driven discovery.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: What are the absolute first things I should do in Scrap Mechanic survival mode?
A1: In survival mode, your first priorities should be putting out the fires on your crashed ship, finding the battery to power it, and then locating the road that leads to the Mechanic's Garage. This will unlock crucial crafting stations and guide your early progression.

### Q2: How do I build a basic vehicle in Scrap Mechanic?
A2: To build a basic vehicle, use the Lift as your building platform. Place a base block, then add a Driver's Seat, a Gas Engine, and wheels attached to bearings. Use the Connect Tool to link the Driver's Seat to the engine and the front bearings for steering, and the engine to the powered wheels. Adjust engine speed and remove the lift to drive.

### Q3: Is there a map in Scrap Mechanic survival mode?
A3: Currently, there is no in-game map in Scrap Mechanic survival mode. Navigation relies on landmarks and following roads. Community reports suggest using items like lamps from scrap towns to create your own directional markers to help you find your way back to your base.

### Q4: What's the best way to learn new building techniques in Scrap Mechanic?
A4: The best way to learn is through experimentation! Don't be afraid to try different combinations of blocks, parts, and interactive components. The in-game Mechanic's Handbook provides details on each part, and watching community videos or tutorials can offer inspiration and advanced techniques for your **Scrap Mechanic beginner guide** journey.
