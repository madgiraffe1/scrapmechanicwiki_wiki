### The Essential Steps of Scrap Mechanic Farming Basics

Before you can enjoy a bountiful harvest, you need to understand the fundamental mechanics of growing crops. The process begins with preparing the soil, planting seeds, and ensuring your crops receive adequate water. These initial steps are straightforward, but mastering them forms the foundation of all successful farming endeavors in *Scrap Mechanic*.

The game provides a helpful tutorial billboard near the crash site's pond, guiding new players through the initial stages. You'll find pre-placed soil mounds, seeds, and a bucket, demonstrating the starting setup. Let's break down the basic workflow:

### Optimizing Your Farm: Growth Times and Advanced Techniques

Maximizing your yield and efficiency is key to a thriving farm. Understanding crop growth times and employing smart techniques can significantly boost your food production. Each crop has a specific growth duration, which can be halved with the application of fertilizer.

For instance, a Carrot takes 21 in-game hours (which translates to real-time minutes) to grow normally, but only 10.5 hours when fertilized. While the official wiki doesn't list all crop growth times, this example highlights the substantial benefit of using fertilizer. Harvesting typically yields one crop item and between one to three seeds, ensuring a continuous supply for replanting.

Beyond manual watering, consider integrating automated solutions. A Vacuum Pump, for example, can be configured to plant seeds and pick ripe crops, streamlining the harvesting process. For watering, a Water Cannon can cover a larger area, or another Vacuum Pump setup can deliver water precisely. Community reports often highlight complex yet efficient automated farm designs using these tools, allowing players to focus on other survival tasks.

### Understanding and Avoiding Robot Raids on Your Farm

One of the biggest challenges in **Scrap Mechanic farming** is dealing with robot raids. These events are triggered when you plant too many crops in a localized area, leading to an "UNAUTHORIZED FARMING DETECTED!" alert. A red timer will appear, counting down to midnight, at which point hostile robots will spawn and attempt to destroy your precious crops.

Each crop type has an associated "crop value." A raid is triggered when the total crop value in an area reaches at least 10. Crops with a value of 3 or more are considered "high-value" and can escalate raid severity. For example, if you have at least five high-value crops and a total crop value of 80 or more, you'll start seeing tougher enemies like Tapebots and Farmbots.

| :-------- | :---- | :------------------ |

| *Other*   | *Varies* | *Yes/No*            |

*Note: Specific values for all crops are not provided in the source, but the example of Carrot (value 1, not high-value) sets a baseline.*

The intensity of raids can increase on consecutive nights if you continue planting. This "farming intensity" resets only when no new crops are planted for a period. If you trigger a raid, the level is determined when the alert first appears, meaning adding or removing crops afterward won't change the upcoming raid's difficulty. Even if you harvest everything, the raid will still occur, though without targets, it becomes easier to manage.

To strategically avoid higher-level raids with low-value crops, player experience suggests planting between midnight and 6:45 AM and harvesting before 4 AM. This timing allows you to get your yield before the game's second-night farm detection period (between 4 and 5 AM) can register a continuous large farm.

### The Art of Mini-Farming: Safe Harvests Without the Hassle

For those who prefer a less confrontational approach to food production, mini-farming is an invaluable strategy. This method focuses on preventing raids entirely by planting smaller, dispersed groups of crops. The core idea is to keep your total crop value below the raid-triggering threshold within any given "cell" of the game world.

Game cells are 64x64 blocks. To ensure your mini-farms go undetected, they need to be separated by a significant distance, ideally placing each small farm in a different cell. While farms near cell boundaries can be closer, the key is to ensure they are recognized as separate entities by the game's raid detection system.

Regularly taking a break from planting for at least one night is crucial to reset the farming intensity. Ignoring this can lead to an accidental raid trigger, even if you haven't recently expanded your farm. If a raid is accidentally triggered, fertilizing your crops for a quick harvest can be a lifesaver, allowing you to collect your goods before the robots arrive. Even if you harvest, the raid will still happen, but it will typically be a manageable Level 1 raid with just a few Totebots.

While mini-farming largely eliminates raid threats, you still need to be wary of wandering Totebots and Haybots. These robots might accidentally stumble upon your crops if they spawn nearby or are chasing a Woc. Fortifying your mini-farms, even with simple defenses, can provide peace of mind. For ultimate security, some players recommend establishing farms on inaccessible mountain tops, such as those surrounding the Farmers Hideout, ensuring robots cannot easily reach them.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Scrap Mechanic Farming

### Q1: How do I start farming in Scrap Mechanic Survival Mode?
A1: To begin **Scrap Mechanic farming**, place a Soil Bag on the ground, plant seeds into the mound, then water the newly planted crops using a Water Bucket. After some time, they will grow and can be harvested. You can find basic supplies and a tutorial billboard near the crash site's pond.

### Q2: What triggers robot raids on my farm?
A2: Robot raids are triggered when you plant too many crops in a concentrated area, specifically when the total "crop value" in a game cell (64x64 blocks) reaches 10 or more. High-value crops (value 3+) can also increase the raid level and attract tougher robots like Tapebots and Farmbots if there are at least five present and the total value exceeds 80.

### Q3: Can I automate the farming process in Scrap Mechanic?
A3: Yes, you can automate various aspects of **Scrap Mechanic farming**. Vacuum Pumps can be used for both planting seeds and harvesting ripe crops. Water Cannons or Vacuum Pumps can also be rigged to automatically water your plants, significantly reducing manual labor and increasing efficiency for larger farms.

### Q4: What is mini-farming, and how does it help avoid raids?
A4: Mini-farming is a strategy where you plant small groups of crops far apart, ensuring each group's "crop value" remains below the raid-triggering threshold within any single 64x64 block game cell. This dispersion prevents the game from detecting an "UNAUTHORIZED FARMING" situation, allowing you to grow food without constant robot attacks. Remember to periodically take a break from planting to reset farming intensity.
