## The Short Answer on the Scrap Mechanic Release Date

The original Scrap Mechanic release date on Steam was January 19, 2016, when Axolot Games launched the game into Early Access. That date is the safest baseline for the game's public PC launch because it comes from the official Steam store listing collected for this project. The same listing identifies Scrap Mechanic as a creative survival sandbox built around machines, vehicles, interactive parts, and player-made engineering.

There is a second date that players now search for: the full 1.0 release timing. The project source review recorded that the 1.0 "Drilling Thunder" update was reported for July 24, 2026, while the Steam page was still presenting the game as Early Access at the time of retrieval on July 16, 2026. That means players should separate the Early Access launch date from the full-release target. One explains when the public Steam journey began; the other explains when the long-running Early Access period is expected to move into a finished 1.0 milestone.

For practical purposes, use this article as a planning guide, not as a substitute for checking the live Steam page before purchase. Release status can change quickly around a major update window, and the official store page is the best place to confirm the current state, supported features, and latest public messaging.

## Early Access vs Full Release

Early Access does not mean the game is unavailable. It means the game is sold and playable while development continues toward a fuller version. For Scrap Mechanic, that distinction matters because many players have already spent years building, testing, and playing Survival Mode. The original release date is real, but it does not necessarily mean every planned feature was present on day one.

When checking the Steam page, look for three pieces of information:

- The current store status, including whether the page still displays Early Access language.
- The update news and announcements near the top of the page.
- The developer's description of what is included now versus what is planned next.

This is especially important near a reported 1.0 launch. Store copy, reviews, community posts, and news items may update at different times. If you are deciding whether to start a new save, return to an old world, or wait for the complete update, use official store and developer channels as the final source.
